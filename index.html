Understanding DPI and Sensitivity

DPI (Dots Per Inch) and in-game sensitivity are crucial settings that determine how your mouse movements translate to cursor or crosshair movement in games. Finding the right balance is essential for precision aiming and consistent performance.

DPI (Dots Per Inch)

DPI measures how many pixels your cursor moves per inch of mouse movement. Higher DPI means faster cursor movement for the same physical distance.

In-Game Sensitivity

This multiplier determines how your mouse input translates to in-game movement. Different games use different sensitivity scales.

eDPI (Effective DPI)

eDPI = DPI × In-game sensitivity. This is a standardized measure that allows for comparison across different setups and games.

cm/360°

The physical distance you need to move your mouse to complete a 360° turn in-game. Lower values mean higher sensitivity.
